# Marketing Performance Analytics

## Overview
Use this skill to turn marketing data into decisions. Focus on business-linked metrics, comparison context, and a repeatable diagnostic workflow rather than vanity reporting.

## When to Use
Use this skill when:
- A team needs weekly, monthly, or quarterly marketing reporting.
- Channel performance is changing and the cause is unclear.
- KPI selection is inconsistent across email, paid, social, SEO, content, and pipeline reporting.
- Forecasts, attribution choices, or optimization priorities need a defensible framework.

Do not use this skill for:
- Brand strategy without measurement requirements.
- Creative direction that is not tied to performance data.

## Quick Reference

### Core reporting principles
- Lead with metrics tied to business objectives.
- Show trends, not isolated snapshots.
- Add context: prior period, target, and benchmark.
- Group metrics by funnel stage or business question.
- Keep summary dashboards to one screen; move detail to an appendix.
- Match update cadence to decision cadence.

### Recommended cadence
| Cadence | Focus |
|---|---|
| Daily | Paid pacing, anomalies, disapprovals |
| Weekly | Channel review, winners vs. underperformers, immediate actions |
| Bi-weekly | Creative refreshes and active tests |
| Monthly | Full performance review, forecast update, new optimization opportunities |
| Quarterly | Channel mix, budget allocation, strategic learnings |

## Reporting Workflow
1. Define the business question.
2. Pick the smallest KPI set that answers it.
3. Pull current period, prior period, target, and benchmark.
4. Segment by channel, campaign, audience, or funnel stage.
5. Identify the bottleneck before recommending fixes.
6. Convert findings into actions, owners, and next review date.

### Recommended report formats
| Report | Include |
|---|---|
| Weekly | Top 3 metric changes, what worked, what needs attention, this week's priorities |
| Monthly | Executive summary, KPI dashboard, channel summaries, campaign results, budget vs. plan, next-month recommendations |
| Quarterly / QBR | Performance vs. goals, YTD trajectory, channel ROI, market context, strategic recommendations, budget shifts, key learnings |

## Diagnostic Workflow
Use this sequence when performance drops or stalls:
1. **Identify** the underperforming metric versus target or benchmark.
2. **Locate** the funnel stage: awareness, interest, consideration, conversion, or retention.
3. **Diagnose** the likely cause: audience, message, creative, offer, timing, UX, or technical issue.
4. **Prioritize** fixes by impact and effort.
5. **Test** one variable at a time.
6. **Measure** against a predefined success metric.
7. **Scale or iterate** based on results.

### Optimization levers by funnel stage
| Funnel stage | Common signal | Primary levers |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Low reach or impressions | Budget, targeting, channel mix, creative format |
| Interest | Low CTR or engagement | Headlines, hooks, creative, audience fit |
| Consideration | High bounce or low time on page | Landing page relevance, speed, UX, content |
| Conversion | Low conversion rate | Offer, CTA, form length, trust signals, layout |
| Retention | Low repeat engagement or high churn | Onboarding, nurture, product experience, support |

### Prioritization matrix
- High impact, low effort: do now.
- High impact, high effort: plan and staff.
- Low impact, low effort: do only if capacity exists.
- Low impact, high effort: deprioritize.

## Trend, Forecast, and Attribution Guidance

### Trend analysis checklist
- Use at least 8-12 data points when possible.
- Look for direction, inflection points, seasonality, anomalies, and leading indicators.
- Overlay launches, pricing changes, budget changes, or market events.
- Form hypotheses, then design tests to validate causation.

### Forecasting options
| Approach | Best for | Caution |
|---|---|---|
| Linear projection | Stable metrics | Breaks quickly under changing conditions |
| Moving average | Smoothing noisy periods | Can hide true inflection points |
| Year-over-year pattern | Seasonal businesses | Needs adjustment for growth or market change |
| Funnel math | Lead-to-revenue planning | Depends on stable conversion assumptions |
| Scenario modeling | Planning under uncertainty | Requires clear assumptions for each case |

Forecasts should be presented as ranges. Anything built on fewer than 12 data points should be marked low confidence.

### Attribution models
| Model | Best use | Main limitation |
|---|---|---|
| Last touch | Final conversion trigger analysis | Undervalues awareness |
| First touch | Top-of-funnel source analysis | Ignores nurture and closing influence |
| Linear | Shared credit across touchpoints | Treats all touches as equal |
| Time decay | Heavier weight on recent touches | Can understate early influence |
| Position-based | Balanced B2B default | Weighting is still arbitrary |
| Data-driven | High-volume programs | Requires enough conversion data |

Practical default:
- Start with last touch if no model exists.
- Compare first touch and last touch to separate awareness from conversion impact.
- Use position-based as a reasonable B2B middle ground.
- Treat all attribution as directional, not absolute truth.

## Common Mistakes
- Reporting vanity metrics without connecting them to pipeline or revenue.
- Comparing current results without prior-period, target, or benchmark context.
- Optimizing a channel in isolation from the rest of the funnel.
- Ending tests early or changing multiple variables at once.
- Presenting precise forecasts without confidence ranges.
- Treating attribution output as fact instead of a model with gaps.
