# Composition & Framing Terms

Quick reference for shot sizes, angles, and compositional grammar for text-to-image prompt engineering.

## Shot Sizes

- **ECU (Extreme Close-Up)** — Eye, mouth, detail; maximum intimacy, abstracting
- **CU (Close-Up)** — Face fills frame; emotion, reaction, texture
- **MCU (Medium Close-Up)** — Head and shoulders; interviews, connection
- **MS (Medium Shot)** — Waist up; body language plus face, conversational
- **WS (Wide Shot)** — Full body in environment; context, spatial relationships
- **EWS (Extreme Wide Shot)** — Subject dwarfed by setting; scale, isolation, grandeur
- **Establishing shot** — Opens a scene; location, time, mood, geography

## Angles

- **Eye-level** — Neutral, objective, no power dynamic implied
- **Low angle** — Subject appears dominant, heroic, imposing
- **High angle** — Subject appears vulnerable, small, subordinate
- **Dutch angle** — Tilted horizon; tension, unease, disorientation
- **Overhead** — 45° above; fashion, food, flat-lay adjacent
- **Bird's-eye** — Looking straight down; maps, godlike detachment
- **Worm's-eye** — Looking straight up; towering structures, awe

## Framing Grammar

- **Rule of thirds** — Subject at intersection of 3×3 grid; balanced, dynamic
- **Golden ratio / Fibonacci spiral** — Mathematical proportion; organic, classical harmony
- **Leading lines** — Converging lines draw eye to subject; roads, fences, architecture
- **Negative space** — Empty area around subject; minimalism, breathing room, emphasis
- **Symmetry** — Mirror balance; formality, calm, precision, surreal
- **Asymmetry** — Intentional imbalance; dynamism, tension, organic feel
- **Balance** — Visual weight distribution; formal (symmetrical) or informal (asymmetrical)
- **Figure-ground** — Clear subject vs background separation; readability, pop
- **Depth layers** — Foreground, midground, background; dimensionality, parallax, immersion
