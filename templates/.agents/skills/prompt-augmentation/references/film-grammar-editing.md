# Film Grammar & Editing Reference

Domain-specific terms for text-to-video prompt augmentation — editing language and shot sequencing.

## Cut Types

- **match cut** — Visual or thematic similarity bridges two shots
- **jump cut** — Sudden temporal leap within same framing; jarring
- **smash cut** — Abrupt transition between contrasting scenes
- **L-cut** — Audio from next scene begins before visual cut
- **J-cut** — Visual of next scene begins before audio transitions
- **cross-cutting / parallel editing** — Alternating between two simultaneous actions
- **hard cut** — Straight, immediate switch with no transition effect
- **cut on action** — Edit during movement to mask the transition

## Transition Types

- **dissolve** — Gradual blend from one shot into the next
- **cross-dissolve** — Overlapping fade suggesting passage of time or connection
- **fade in** — Image emerges from black (or white)
- **fade out** — Image dissolves to black (or white)
- **fade to black** — Conventional scene-ending transition
- **wipe** — One frame pushes the previous frame off screen
- **iris in/out** — Circular mask expands or contracts to reveal/hide frame

## Structural Units

- **montage** — Compressed sequence of shots condensing time or information
- **sequence** — Series of shots forming a unified narrative segment
- **scene** — Continuous dramatic unit in a single time and place

## Shot Sequencing Grammar

Standard progression for narrative clarity:

1. **establishing shot** — Sets location, time, and spatial context
2. **wide shot** — Shows full subject and environment relationship
3. **medium shot** — Frames subject waist-up; conversational distance
4. **close-up** — Fills frame with face or detail; emotional intimacy
5. **extreme close-up / detail** — Isolates a specific element or texture
6. **reaction shot** — Captures subject's response to preceding action
7. **cutaway** — Brief insert of related but off-axis content
8. **insert shot** — Close-up of a significant prop or detail in scene

## Narrative Flow Markers

- **slow reveal** — Gradual disclosure of information building curiosity
- **building tension** — Ascending rhythm of shorter shots and tighter framing
- **emotional climax** — Peak dramatic moment; often close-up or slow motion
- **resolution** — Decompression after climax; longer shots, wider framing
- **cold open** — In-media-res start before credits or establishing context
- **button** — Final beat that punctuates or recontextualizes a scene
