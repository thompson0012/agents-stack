# Optics & Camera Terms

Quick reference for lens, aperture, and optical terminology used in text-to-image prompt engineering.

## Lens Types

- **24mm** — Ultra-wide; expansive scenes, architecture, environmental storytelling
- **35mm** — Wide-standard; street, documentary, natural perspective
- **50mm** — Normal; closest to human eye, versatile all-purpose focal length
- **85mm** — Short telephoto; classic portrait lens, flattering compression
- **135mm** — Telephoto; tight portraits, subject isolation, background compression
- **Macro** — Extreme close-up; 1:1 or greater magnification, fine detail
- **Fisheye** — Ultra-wide 180° view; spherical distortion, experimental
- **Anamorphic** — Widescreen cinema lens; oval bokeh, horizontal flare, cinematic feel
- **Vintage glass** — Older uncoated lenses; flaring, softer contrast, character imperfections

## Aperture & Depth of Field

- **f/1.4** — Very wide; razor-thin DoF, maximum bokeh, low-light champion
- **f/2.8** — Wide; shallow DoF, subject separation, popular zoom aperture
- **f/5.6** — Moderate; balanced DoF, group portraits, general use
- **f/8** — Sharp; deep DoF, landscape sweet spot, edge-to-edge sharpness
- **f/16** — Very deep; maximum DoF, hyperfocal, front-to-back sharpness
- **Shallow DoF** — Narrow in-focus plane; isolates subject, blurs background/foreground
- **Deep DoF** — Wide in-focus zone; everything sharp, landscape, architectural
- **Bokeh** — Aesthetic quality of out-of-focus highlights; creamy, nervous, swirly
- **Circle of confusion** — Largest blur spot still perceived as sharp; defines DoF limits

## Perspective & Distortion

- **Focal compression** — Telephoto flattens depth; stacking background toward subject
- **Perspective distortion** — Wide-angle exaggerates near/far size differences
- **Tilt-shift** — Selective plane focus; miniature effect, architectural correction
- **Lens distortion** — Barrel (wide), pincushion (tele), mustache (complex curvatures)
