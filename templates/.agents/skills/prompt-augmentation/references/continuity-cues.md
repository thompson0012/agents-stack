# Continuity Cues Reference

Domain-specific terms for text-to-video prompt augmentation — spatial, temporal, and visual consistency language.

## Spatial Continuity

- **180-degree rule** — Camera stays on one side of an imaginary line between subjects
- **eyeline match** — Character's gaze direction aligns with off-screen subject in next shot
- **screen direction** — Consistent left-to-right or right-to-left movement across cuts
- **crossing the line** — Deliberate violation of 180° rule; disorients the viewer
- **axis of action** — The imaginary line defining spatial relationships in a scene
- **shot/reverse shot** — Alternating views matching conversational eyelines

## Temporal Continuity

- **match on action** — Cut continues the same physical motion across two angles
- **temporal ellipsis** — Omitted time between shots; jump forward without disruption
- **flashback** — Interruption to show earlier events; visual shift signals time change
- **flashforward** — Jump ahead in timeline; preview of future events
- **real-time continuity** — No time compression; events unfold at natural duration
- **compressed time** — Abbreviated depiction of a longer process

## Lighting & Prop Continuity

- **consistent key light** — Maintained primary light direction and quality across cuts
- **consistent color temperature** — Unchanged white balance and tint between shots
- **prop continuity** — Objects remain in same state and position across scene
- **wardrobe continuity** — Clothing and appearance remain consistent within timeline
- **practical light** — On-screen light sources that must remain spatially consistent

## Geography Consistency

- **established geography** — Clear spatial layout communicated early and maintained
- **consistent background** — Environment does not shift between matched angles
- **spatial anchoring** — Recomposing shots reference known landmarks or layout
- **interior logic** — Room layout and object placement remains coherent scene-to-scene

## Shot Scale Progression

- **logical zoom** — Each successive shot is closer or wider by a noticeable degree
- **size consistency** — Matched subject size when cutting between angles
- **step-in pattern** — Establishing → wide → medium → close-up; natural zoom-in
- **step-out pattern** — Close-up → medium → wide; context-building reveal
- **alternating scale** — Intercutting between tight and wide for contrast and rhythm

## Reverse-Angle Conventions

- **clean reverse** — Directly opposing angle without set wall obstruction
- **dirty reverse** — Over-the-shoulder with foreground element from opposite side
- **complimentary angle** — Off-axis reverse giving slight variation from direct opposition
- **cutaway for reorientation** — Brief insert shot resetting spatial understanding
