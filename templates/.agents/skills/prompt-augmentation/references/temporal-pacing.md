# Temporal Pacing Reference

Domain-specific terms for text-to-video prompt augmentation — speed, duration, and frame rate language.

## Speed Modifiers

- **slow motion** — Playback slower than capture rate; emphasizes detail
- **ultra slow motion** — Extreme deceleration revealing micro-events
- **time-lapse** — Long duration compressed into brief playback
- **hyperlapse** — Time-lapse with significant camera movement between frames
- **real-time** — 1:1 capture-to-playback speed; naturalistic
- **bullet time** — Array of cameras freezing a moment in 3D space
- **speed ramp** — Dynamic shift from fast to slow (or vice versa) within a shot
- **undercrank** — Fewer frames captured; playback appears faster
- **overcrank** — More frames captured; playback appears slower
- **freeze frame** — Single frame held; suspends time completely

## Duration Cues

- **brief glimpse** — Sub-one-second flash; fleeting, impressionistic
- **quick beat** — 1–2 second shot; punchy, rhythmic
- **lingering shot** — Extended hold on a subject; contemplative
- **sustained take** — Long unbroken shot building immersion
- **epic sweep** — Grand, extended shot covering vast change or geography
- **held beat** — Pause after action for emotional resonance

## Motion Blur Intensity

- **heavy motion blur** — Smooth streaks; implies speed and fluidity
- **light motion blur** — Subtle trails; natural quick movement
- **sharp / crisp** — Minimal blur; clean, frozen-moment quality
- **freeze-frame** — Zero blur; absolute temporal stillness
- **stutter step** — Stepped or staccato motion; skipped frames effect
- **dreamy blur** — Soft, ethereal trailing; romantic or surreal feel

## Loopability

- **seamless loop** — End frame matches start; infinite replay with no visible join
- **ping-pong** — Plays forward then reverses; natural-feeling loop
- **one-shot** — Single-play narrative; not designed to loop
- **hold loop** — Static or near-static frame that extends indefinitely
- **crossfade loop** — End dissolves into beginning for smooth cycling

## Frame Rate References

- **cinematic 24fps** — Standard film look; natural motion blur, dream-like cadence
- **broadcast 30fps** — TV/sports standard; slightly crisper than cinematic
- **smooth 60fps** — Hyper-fluid; common in gaming, sports, some digital content
- **high frame rate 120fps** — Very smooth; used for 4×–5× slow motion source
- **ultra 240fps** — Professional slow motion capture rate
- **extreme 960fps+** — Ultra-slow motion; reveals events invisible to the eye
