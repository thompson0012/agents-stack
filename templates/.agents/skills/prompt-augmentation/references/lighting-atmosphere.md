# Lighting & Atmosphere Terms

Quick reference for light sources, studio setups, modifiers, and mood for text-to-image prompt engineering.

## Natural Light

- **Golden hour** — Warm low sun just after sunrise / before sunset; soft, directional, flattering
- **Blue hour** — Twilight before sunrise / after sunset; cool, moody, ambient glow
- **Overcast** — Cloud-diffused sunlight; even, soft, low-contrast, no hard shadows
- **Direct sunlight** — Unobstructed sun; hard shadows, high contrast, specular highlights
- **Backlight** — Light source behind subject; rim light, silhouette, halo effect

## Studio Setups

- **Three-point** — Key + fill + back/rim; standard controlled lighting, dimension
- **Rembrandt** — Key 45° above and side; triangle highlight on shadow cheek, dramatic
- **Butterfly / paramount** — Key directly above; butterfly shadow under nose, glamour
- **Split** — Light from 90° side; half face lit, half shadow, dramatic duality
- **Loop** — Key slightly off-center; small nose shadow loop, classic portrait
- **Clamshell** — Top and bottom lights; even glamour, minimized shadows, beauty

## Modifiers

- **Softbox** — Large diffused panel; soft even wrap-around light, flattering
- **Beauty dish** — Shallow reflector dish; contrasty but soft, specular pop, fashion
- **Umbrella** — Shoot-through or reflective; broad soft spill, budget-friendly
- **Gobo** — Go-between; pattern projector, window blinds, tree shadows
- **Snoot** — Narrow tube; spotlight, hard circle, hair light accent
- **Grid / honeycomb** — Directional control; prevents spill, focused beam
- **Barn doors** — Flaps on light; shape and direct beam, flag edges
- **Flag** — Opaque panel; blocks light, creates shadow, negative fill
- **Cookie / cucaloris** — Cut-out pattern board; dappled shadow, foliage effect

## Mood

- **Chiaroscuro** — Strong light-dark contrast; Renaissance painting, Caravaggio
- **High-key** — Predominantly bright tones; airy, optimistic, clean
- **Low-key** — Predominantly dark tones; mysterious, dramatic, intimate
- **Noir** — Hard shadows, venetian blind patterns; crime, mystery, cynicism
- **Dramatic** — Strong directional, high contrast; emotional intensity
- **Naturalistic** — Mimics real-world light; believable, motivated, understated
- **Motivated** — Styled to appear from in-scene source; window, lamp, screen
- **Practical** — Visible on-set light sources; lamps, candles, neon, screens
