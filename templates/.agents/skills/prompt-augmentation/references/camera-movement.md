# Camera Movement Reference

Domain-specific terms for text-to-video prompt augmentation — motion and camera language.

## Subject Motion

- **static** — Subject remains still; no perceivable movement
- **walk** — Natural ambulatory motion at standard pace
- **run** — Accelerated locomotion with fuller stride
- **drift** — Slow, directionless lateral movement
- **float** — Buoyant, gravity-defying gentle motion
- **hover** — Suspended in place with subtle oscillation
- **crawl** — Low, slow ground-level progression
- **sprint** — Maximum-effort explosive running
- **glide** — Smooth, effortless continuous motion
- **stagger** — Unsteady, irregular forward movement
- **lunge** — Sudden forward thrust of the body
- **pivot** — Rotation around a fixed point

## Camera Motion Types

### Panning & Tilting
- **pan (left/right)** — Horizontal rotation on a fixed vertical axis
- **whip pan** — Fast, snapping horizontal pan with motion blur
- **tilt (up/down)** — Vertical rotation on a fixed horizontal axis

### Dolly & Tracking
- **dolly in** — Camera moves physically toward the subject
- **dolly out** — Camera moves physically away from the subject
- **truck (left/right)** — Camera moves laterally on a track
- **tracking shot** — Camera follows subject movement in parallel

### Vertical Movement
- **pedestal (up/down)** — Vertical shift of the entire camera
- **crane up** — Camera rises via crane arm from low to high
- **crane down** — Camera descends via crane arm from high to low
- **boom (up/down)** — Small vertical shift via boom arm

### Stabilized & Unstabilized
- **steadicam** — Smooth floating motion via body-mount stabilizer
- **gimbal** — Electronically stabilized fluid movement
- **handheld** — Intentional shake and organic imperfection
- **jib** — Sweeping arc movement on a counter-weighted arm

### Aerial
- **drone aerial** — Wide overhead or sweeping shot from UAV
- **drone orbit** — Circular flight path around a subject

## Special Moves & Effects

- **rack focus** — Shift focal plane from one subject to another
- **pull focus** — Adjust focus distance during a shot
- **zoom in** — Optically magnify without camera translation
- **zoom out** — Optically widen without camera translation
- **crash zoom** — Abrupt, aggressive zoom with motion blur
- **snap zoom** — Instant, jarring zoom transition
- **dolly zoom / Vertigo effect** — Simultaneous dolly + zoom creating a warping perspective shift
- **orbital** — Camera circles 360° around the subject
- **push-in** — Gradual, tension-building approach toward subject
- **pull-out** — Revealing retreat from subject to context
- **reveal** — Camera movement that unveils previously hidden information
